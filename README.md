**Musicatea** is a modern Arabic music website dedicated to presenting music theory, notation, history, and educational resources in a clear, elegant, and accessible way.

The platform is built as a static site with a strong visual identity inspired by Arabic musical heritage. It is designed for students, teachers, performers, researchers, and anyone interested in Arabic music.

## Overview

Musicatea brings together multiple parts of Arabic music education in one place, including:

- **Sheet music library**
- **Interactive maqam pages**
- **Arabic rhythms**
- **Music history**
- **Educational program pages**
- **Arabic-first interface with a refined visual design**

The project aims to make Arabic music knowledge easier to explore, learn, and preserve online.

## Main Sections

### 1. Homepage
The homepage introduces the platform and provides fast access to the main sections of the site.

### 2. Sheet Music Library
A structured library of downloadable music sheets with filtering and search tools.

### 3. Maqamat
Dedicated maqam pages with interactive scale presentation and educational content.

### 4. Interactive Scale
An interactive maqam experience that allows users to explore scale notes and listen to audio playback.

### 5. Rhythms
A section focused on Arabic rhythmic patterns and learning resources.

### 6. Music History
A historical overview of Arabic music, its development, and major figures.

### 7. Ensemble / Program Pages
Pages dedicated to music education initiatives and performance projects.

## Project Goals

Musicatea was created to:

- make Arabic music knowledge more accessible online
- provide a visually strong and culturally aligned educational platform
- support music students, teachers, and independent learners
- combine theory, listening, notation, and history in one website
- preserve and present Arabic music in a structured digital form
